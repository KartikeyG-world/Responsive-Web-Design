Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was an American agronomist, humanitarian, and Nobel laureate who has been called "the father of the Green Revolution." His work in developing high-yield, disease-resistant wheat varieties led to massive increases in food production and helped alleviate famine in several countries, especially in Asia and Latin America.

His efforts earned him numerous awards, including the Nobel Peace Prize in 1970, and his legacy continues to influence modern agriculture and food security programs globally.
